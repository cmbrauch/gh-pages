I attended school at Misericorida University where I played Division 3 lacrosse. I found a specialty in tournament poker, finding myself consistently in the top 1% of players in terms of Return of Investment. Poker taught me how to make my decisions based on incomplete information, but more importantly taught me to become extremely logical in my decisions, something that I hope will translate well with coding!

    In 2010, I also learned how to become a poker dealer, a profession that I still do to this day. Being a good poker dealer requires that I am good at multitasking; having an efficiently-run game while concurrently offering above-average customer service. It is certainly a skill to keep a player in line and then have him tip for the rulings you enforce!
